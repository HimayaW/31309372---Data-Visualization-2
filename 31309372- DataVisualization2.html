GLOBAL ASYLUM SEEKERS (2006 - 2020)

Asylum seekers are immigrants, who are displaced to live in another place against their will, mostly because of war or other factors harming them or their family (UN Refugee Agency, 2021). This visualization focuses on which countries are these asylum seekers originated from, to which countries they move to and decisions taken by countries of asylum during the years 2006 to 2020.

The decisions taken by countries of asylum includes Recognized decisions, Complementary protection and Rejected decisions.

ASYLUM SEEKERS FROM COUNTRY OF ORIGIN AND ACCEPTED BY COUNTRY OF ASYLUM

The world map here shows the number of applications made by asylum seekers acccording to their country of origin. Countries like Afghanistan, Iraq, Ukraine and Venezuela are some countries that had the highest asylum seekers (more than 150000) throughout the years of 2006 and 2020.

Countries like the United States, United Kingdom, Canada and Australia have the lowest asylum seeker applications, which shows that most asylum seekers are originated from developing countries which had wars, which is not the case in developed countries. The highest number of asylum applicants were in 2015, which was the year of Europe Refugee Crisis, according to BBC(2016).

The above stacked bar graph demonstrates the total number of applicants that were accepted into the country of asylum. The top 10 countries that have accepted applicants vary throughout the years of 2006 and 2020. The highest accepting countries of asylum include both developed and developing countries. Acceptance of applicants are dividing into 2 categories, applicants that are recognized as asylums and applicants that are provided complementary protection. Most applicants are recognized asylum applicants. Germany has accepted the highest number of applicants continuously in the recent years. Russia has accepted the highest number of applicants with complementary protection.

TOP 10 COUNTRIES WITH HIGHEST APPLICATION REJECTIONS

The top 10 countries with the highest rejections (in absolute terms) in total are from Germany, France, United States of America, United Kingdom, Italy, Sweden, Australia, South Africa, Greece and Belgium. These have the highest rejections because of they have the largest number of applicants as well (most of them being developed countries). Some reasons as to why these countries refuse asylum seekers is because of fears in economic competiton, persistent rift within a country's society and the possibility of terror attacks from occuring.

Overall, Germany has had the highest rejected applicants, with 279426 applicants rejected in 2017 alone. Years with lower rejections could also be due to lower number of applicants. In year 2020, the pandemic has prevented people from migrating or moving from one country to another, which reduces the number of asylum applicants.

DISTRIBUTION OF RECOGNITION RATES THROUGHOUT THE YEARS 2006 TO 2020

Recognition rate is the percentage at which the country of asylum takes in asylum seekers that are recognized by the government over the total number of applicants (both accepted and rejected applicants) (UN Refugee Agency, 2009). Recognition rate is an internationally recognized and agreed methodology used to calculate the percentage of applicants accepted with refugee convention.

This boxplot shows the distribution of recognition rates throughout the years 2006 and 2020. As demonstrated the distribution of recognition rates changes from year to year (length of the boxplot). The median recognition rate, shown in white, fluctuates continuously throughout the years but it has an overall upward trend with the highest median recognition rate in 2020 and a percentage of 68.67%. The lowest median recognition percentage was 36.13% in the year 2012.

RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN PERCENTAGE OF REJECTED APPLICANTS AGAINST PERCENTAGE OF CLOSED APPLICANTS

The percentage of rejected applicants is calculated by dividing the total number of rejected applicants over total number of decisions made. Closed spplication percentage is calculated by divding the total number of closed applications over the total number of decisions made. Closed application decisions refer to the applications made by asylum seekers who withdraw their application, applicants who haven't attended the interview or applicants who have died before the interview. In UNCHR statistics these are referred to as "Otherwise Closed" decisions.

This scatter plot shows that there is a negative relationship between the percentage of applicants rejected and the percentage of closed applications. This means that when the percentage of rejected applicats (Rejection Rate) increased the percentage of closed applications fall and vice versa. This explains that if the country of asylum rejects more and more applicants as a percentage of their total decisions, the percentage of asylum applicants who withdraw their application or become absent in their interviews decrease. This maybe because withdrawing applications and not attending interviews increases the chances of rejected applications whenever they apply the next time.
